Guiding values and how they were embodied in the UI

My UI was guided by values of calmness, simplicity, and relaxation. I wanted users to feel peaceful, like they were at the beach. The design uses soft blue colours, gentle bubble movement, and ocean sounds to create this mood. I avoided text so that the interface feels more natural. The bubbles, sound, and motion work together to communicate without needing any words.

Feedback

The feedback in my UI comes from both sound and movement. Each bubble plays a different musical note using Tone.js, which makes the experience more interesting and fun. The different tones make the interaction feel more dynamic and playful, while still matching the calm theme.

Information Design

The information design in my project is very minimal. I wanted to remove distractions and only keep elements that add to the calm experience. Instead of using text or icons, I use movement, sound, and colour to give users information. For example, the way the bubbles float upward and disappear suggests that they can be interacted with. The design lets users explore and discover naturally without needing words or labels.

Mapping

The mapping of interactions in my UI is simple and direct. When users click a bubble something happens, it plays a different musical note each time. The connection between action and response is clear, so users understand the interaction right away. The position and movement of the bubbles also guide the eye and invite exploration, which helps the user learn what to do just by observing the interface.

Learnability

The UI is easy to learn because it teaches through experience instead of text. The popup at the beginning gives a short introduction, but after that, users can understand how it works just by interacting. The consistent reactions like sound and motion help users quickly pick up what happens when they engage with the bubbles. After one or two clicks, most users can enjoy the interaction without needing more guidance.

Significant changes since the work in progress milestone

Since my earlier version, I made several big changes. I added more bubbles, changed the background to a realistic beach image, and made the bubbles bigger, more bolder and easier to see. I also added Tone.js so that each bubble plays a different note when clicked. I really liked the addition of Tone,js, I felt like this update made the UI more interactive and immersive. The new popup message also helps first-time users understand the experience better.

Production timeline and development time

Step 1: Concept & Visual Planning
Started by sketching the layout and look of the Chill Bubble UI. The plan focused on a clean, open screen with floating bubbles in the centre and a calm ocean-inspired background. The main goal was to create a relaxing and wordless experience, guided by colour, movement, and sound rather than text.

Step 2: Base Structure
Built the initial structure using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Created one main bubble in the middle of the screen that responded to clicks with a looping ocean breeze track to set the calm, ambient mood.

Step 3: Visual & Interaction Refinement
Expanded from a single bubble to multiple floating bubbles. Improved the visual design by making bubbles larger, slower, and more visible. Adjusted the animation and background to fit the beach theme better. Added subtle glow effects and smoother transitions to make the experience more immersive.

Step 4: Sound Design & Tone.js Integration
Introduced Tone.js to make each bubble play a unique musical note when clicked. Balanced the Tone.js synth sounds with the background ocean ambience. This made the interaction more dynamic and engaging while still keeping the relaxing tone.

Step 5: User Experience & Feedback
Added a popup message that appears when users first load the page, explaining the interaction briefly. This helped with learnability without breaking the no-text aesthetic. Conducted user testing with friends and family to check if the triggers worked and are functioning correctly.

Step 6: Final Polish
Adjusted colours, transparency, and bubble movement speed to enhance visibility and harmony. Final testing focused on ensuring that the sound, visuals, and interaction all worked together to create a peaceful, unified experience.

Aspect that I’m most proud of

I’m most proud of how calm and connected everything feels in the final version. The sounds, colours, and animations all work together to create a relaxing experience. The combination of ocean sounds and musical notes gives it a peaceful and playful feeling. It captures the atmosphere of the beach that inspired me from the beginning.

Most challenging aspect

The most challenging part of this project was finding the right balance between visuals, sound, and interaction. I wanted everything to feel calm and natural, but still interesting enough to keep users engaged. Making the bubbles move smoothly while also reacting to clicks and playing sounds took a lot of testing. It was challenging to make everything work together in harmony, but in the end, it helped the whole experience feel more alive and peaceful.
